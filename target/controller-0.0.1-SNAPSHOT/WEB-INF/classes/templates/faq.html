What is a Sexually Transmitted Disease (STD)?

Any of various diseases or infections that can be transmitted by direct sexual contact including some (as syphilis, gonorrhea, chlamydia, and genital herpes) chiefly spread by sexual means and others (as hepatitis B and AIDS) often contracted by nonsexual means-called also STD.
From Merriam-Webster.com

What are the symptoms of STDs?

Genital herpes (HSV-2) infection and genital warts signs and symptoms may include small red bumps, blisters (vesicles) or open sores (ulcers) in the genital, anal and nearby areas. The symptoms may also include pain or itching around the genital area, buttocks and inner thighs.
Hepatitis signs and symptoms abdominal pain or discomfort, especially in the area of your liver on your right side beneath your lower ribs, loss of appetite, fever, dark urine, muscle or joint pain, itching, yellowing of your skin and the whites of your eyes (jaundice).
Chlamydia signs and symptoms may painful urination, lower abdominal pain, vaginal discharge in women, discharge from the penis in men, pain during sexual intercourse in women, bleeding between periods in women, and testicular pain in men.
Early HIV signs and symptoms may include fever, headache, sore throat, swollen lymph glands, rash, and fatigue.
Gonorrhea symptoms signs and symptoms may include cloudy or bloody discharge from the penis or vagina, pain or burning sensation when urinating, heavy menstrual bleeding or bleeding between periods, painful, swollen testicles, painful bowel movements, and anal itching.

We only kissed, could I have contracted an STD?

Yes, STDs can be transmitted during a kiss. However, this is much less likely than during vaginal, anal or oral sexual contacts. Many STDs can be transmitted through contact with bodily fluids, such as blood or semen. Saliva is not a very probable way for STDs to be transmitted.

Are STDs curable?

It is possible for bacterial STDs to be cured with antibiotics if treatment starts early enough. However, viral STDs cannot be cured, they can be manage symptoms with medications.

Could I have the same STD again?

Yes, for example you may beat a strain of chlamydia and gonorrhea and get infected again by another stain of the same STD. However, others such as herpes and HIV are permanent.

How can I avoid all STDs?

You can greatly reduce your chances by being in a monogamous relationship with a virgin, always using condom and/or dental dam. But, even at these extremes, it still does not provide any guarantees and you should still get tested regularly.

I used a condom, am I ok?
